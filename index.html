Communication Barrier:

CCMS LOUNGE

We chose the CCMS lounge as a communication barrier because it is one of the places in school that creates a barrier in communicating when it's overcrowded. When there's a lot of people in one particular place, there tends to be a large amount of ambient noise from people talking, moving around, and other activities. The noises afore mentioned make it difficult to hear and understand conversations, this sometimes lead to miscommunication. Moreover, overcrowded places often hinder other people to interact with others.

Solution:

Below are some of the solution that we came up with to solve the communication barrier in the CCMS lounge:

  • Add a wall fan near the spot in the lounge such as the tables in the corner where the air from the ceiling fan does not reach.
  • Prohibit students from other department to hang out or stay in our lounge to avoid the place getting overcrowded, resulting to the place getting hotter than it already is.


Communication Barrier:

CBA DEPARTMENT

One of the barriers that can affect our communication would be a physical one. An example is in the college of business and accountancy department. The classrooms there, especially the one near the stairs get so hot in the afternoon. This would be an example of an environment barrier. If the room is too hot, the main focus of the people inside the room will be the heat instead of the discussion that they're having. It could hinder the communication between the students and the teacher. Hot environments can lead to physical discomfort, including sweating, fatigue, and dehydration. These physical sensations can distract individuals from focusing on communication and make it harder for them to engage in conversations effectively.

Solution:

Below are some of the solution that we came up with to solve the communication barrier in the CBA Department:

  • A solution to this barrier would be installments of air condition in the rooms of the college of business and accountancy. This is the most efficient way to address hot environments like the rooms in the CBA department.
  • If feasible, install heat-reflective windowsthat reflect sunlight and heat to reduce the amount of heat entering the room through windows. This can help maintain cooler temperatures indoors, especially during sunny days.
  • Another solution is to encourage the students to stay hydrated by providing easy access to water stations with cold water or installing water coolers in the room.
